ACM

The Association for Computing Machinery is an international learned society for computing. It was founded in 1947 and is the world's largest scientific and educational computing society. The ACM is a non-profit professional membership group, claiming nearly 100,000 student and professional members as of 2019.ACM Chapters Connect Computing Professionals Worldwide ACM fosters growth in the computing community through its more than 180 professional and more than 680 student chapters worldwide. Joining an ACM chapter conveys a profound interest and dedication to the computing discipline. The next step is volunteering in a variety of roles and capacities that can benefit the chapter and advance career opportunities as well. Founded by Richard Hamming in 1947, ACM brings together computing educators, researchers, and professionals to inspire dialogue, share resources, and address the field's challenges.

AISSMS ACM

ACM IOIT Student Chapter was founded on 2019, with a vision to carry forward ACM’s international intentions on a grass root level at AISSMS’ Institute of Information Technology. The motive behind starting this Student Chapter was to instill in everyone an intrigue and passion for learning and innovation in technology. Our Chapter, though only recently established and is one the newer Chapters, aims to expand in quality and quantity, and to be instrumental to every student’s technical growth. Our Chapter focuses on enhancing and boosting every student’s technical fervor by frequently organizing various events throughout the year like Technical Competitions, Eminent Speaker Programs, and Discussions with the chapter members. We are currently a family of 100 members and strive to be an efficient platform for students at an undergraduate level for learning new skills and putting those skills to good use.
